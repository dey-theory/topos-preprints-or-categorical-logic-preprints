Submit a Preprint

Topos Preprints accepts original research in categorical logic, topos theory, type theory, and related areas of mathematics.

Submission Guidelines

We accept papers in the following areas:

  • Topos theory and categorical logic
  • Higher category theory and higher topos theory
  • Type theory and homotopy type theory
  • Categorical semantics and proof theory
  • Sheaf theory and categorical algebraic geometry
  • Effective topos theory and realizability
  • Applications of categorical methods to logic and foundations

Requirements

  • Papers must present original research
  • Mathematical content must be rigorous
  • PDF format preferred
  • Include title, author(s), date, and abstract
  • Papers are reviewed for quality and relevance (typically within 48-72 hours)

How to Submit

Send your preprint to: deyprabahan@gmail.com

Please include in your email:

  • Paper PDF as attachment
  • Title of the paper
  • Author name(s) and affiliation(s) (if any)
  • Brief abstract (can be copied from paper)
  • Any relevant keywords or subject classifications

Note: Topos Preprints is an open archive. By submitting, you agree to make your work publicly available. This does not prevent subsequent journal publication.

About Topos Preprints

Mission

Topos Preprints is an open preprint repository dedicated to categorical logic, topos theory, and related fields. We provide a platform for researchers to share their work freely, without institutional barriers.

Philosophy

Mathematics thrives on open exchange of ideas. Traditional preprint servers often impose institutional requirements that exclude independent researchers and mathematicians from underrepresented regions. Topos Preprints aims to democratize access to mathematical publication while maintaining rigorous standards.

Scope

We accept papers in:

  • Topos theory and categorical logic
  • Higher category theory
  • Type theory and proof assistants
  • Categorical semantics
  • Sheaf theory and descent
  • Effective mathematics and realizability
  • Categorical foundations of mathematics

Quality Control

All submissions are reviewed for mathematical rigor and relevance. We filter out:

  • Papers with fundamental mathematical errors
  • Work that does not engage with the categorical literature
  • Submissions that misuse technical terminology
  • Non-mathematical content

However, we welcome exploratory work, negative results, and papers that challenge conventional perspectives, provided they maintain mathematical standards.
